Guadalupe Martinez – California Revealed's Audiovisual Digitization and Preservation Manager

Full Interview Transcript

Overview

California Revealed is a statewide digitization initiative which helps public libraries, archives, museums, historical societies, and other heritage groups digitize, preserve, and provide online access to archival materials documenting the state's histories, arts, and cultures. It also provides support and assistance for cataloging, community archiving, and K-12 curriculum development, and provides training and support for Memory Labs at public libraries across California.

It began in 2010 as CAVPP as a temporary program funded by the California State Library, and since then has grown in capability, scope, and demand year after year.

As Audiovisual Digitization and Preservation Manager, Guadalupe Martinez manages and oversees California Revealed projects from application review to long-term preservation. This involves coordinating with partner organizations to write basic metadata for and ship their collections, which are then processed and sent to third-party vendors for digitization.

Typical Tasks

Guadalupe is responsible for assessing the feasibility of digitization projects from community heritage group partners, adapting metadata to California Revealed’s standards, and coordinating digitization and preservation with external vendors.

In addition to project management, Guadalupe creates educational resources such as handbooks to demystify digitization processes and technical jargon for community partners.

A specialty of the role involves establishing preservation priorities using the FEMA Natural Risk Index and Healthy Places Index (HPI). These metrics help identify communities most vulnerable to climate risks or social inequities, ensuring that under-resourced communities receive greater archival support and access.

Professional Context

California Revealed collaborates with professional organizations such as the Association of Moving Image Archivists (AMIA) and the Council on Library and Information Resources (CLIR). Staff regularly attend conferences including the Society of American Archivists Annual Meeting and AMIA’s annual conference.

Job Market Outlook

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment for archivists, curators, and museum workers is projected to grow approximately 6% between 2024 and 2034. The growing volume of digital records across government, corporate, and nonprofit sectors is expected to increase demand for professionals who can organize, preserve, and provide access to information.

However, many positions remain dependent on grant funding or temporary contracts, particularly in community archives and smaller cultural institutions.

Technical Skill Snapshot

Audiovisual archivists must understand the preservation, restoration, and access of many media formats including film, magnetic tape, optical media, and digital files. Guadalupe’s early work at the Bay Area Video Coalition involved digitizing formats such as VHS, U-matic, DAT, and open reel tape.

These experiences developed the technical foundation required for managing large-scale audiovisual digitization projects at California Revealed.

Labor Trends

Long-term preservation initiatives often face unstable funding structures. California Revealed has historically been funded by the California State Library but has recently registered as a nonprofit organization to diversify funding opportunities and ensure sustainability.
