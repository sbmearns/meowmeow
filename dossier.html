IS 270 Specialization Dossier

Guadalupe Martinez – California Revealed's Audiovisual Digitization and Preservation Manager

Overview

California Revealed is a statewide digitization initiative that helps public libraries, archives, museums, historical societies, and community heritage organizations digitize, preserve, and provide online access to archival materials documenting California’s histories, arts, and cultures.

The program also supports cataloging work, community archiving projects, K–12 curriculum development, and Memory Labs at public libraries across California.

Originally founded in 2010 as the California Audiovisual Preservation Project (CAVPP), the initiative began as a temporary program funded by the California State Library. Over time it has grown significantly in scope and demand.

Typical Tasks

As Audiovisual Digitization and Preservation Manager, Guadalupe Martinez oversees projects from application review through long-term preservation. This involves coordinating with partner organizations to write basic metadata and arrange shipping of materials.

Once collections are processed they are sent to third-party digitization vendors. After digitization, files are checked for fixity and metadata accuracy before quality control reports are shared with partners.

Partners may also receive physical hard drives of digitized materials. Access copies are hosted online while preservation files are stored on LTO tape with copies stored both in Sacramento and in an off-site vault.

Professional Context

California Revealed collaborates with several professional organizations in the archival and media preservation fields.

  • Association of Moving Image Archivists (AMIA)
  • Council on Library and Information Resources (CLIR)
  • Society of American Archivists (SAA)

Staff regularly attend conferences such as the SAA Annual Meeting and AMIA’s annual conference.

Job Market Outlook

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment for archivists, curators, and museum workers is projected to grow about 6% between 2024 and 2034. This growth is driven in part by increasing volumes of digital records that require management and long-term preservation.

However, many positions remain dependent on grant funding or temporary contracts. Community archives and digital preservation programs are expanding, but publicly funded cultural institutions can experience fluctuations depending on political priorities and funding availability.

Technical Skill Snapshot

Audiovisual archivists must understand the preservation, restoration, and access of many media formats including film, magnetic video and audiotape, optical media, and digital files.

Guadalupe developed many of these skills through work with the Bay Area Video Coalition, digitizing formats such as VHS, U-matic, DAT, and open reel tape.
